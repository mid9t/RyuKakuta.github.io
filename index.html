About

I work on machine learning systems where explanation matters as much as prediction. I’m most interested in understanding failure modes—when models break, why they break, and how to surface causes in a way that changes decisions.design and developmentengineering, and system design—especially where technical rigor directly shapes real business decisions.

Currently, I’m a Data Science undergraduate at UC Berkeley. In my recent work, I built an automated root cause analysis system for sales forecast anomalies, combining traditional models with LLM-based agents to reason over internal business data and external events. The focus wasn’t accuracy alone, but enforcing data correctness, interpretable attribution, and system reliability under real-world constraints.

Across projects, I tend to work end-to-end: cleaning and joining messy data, engineering features, choosing models for the right reasons, and designing orchestration logic that can survive unreliable inputs. I care a lot about understanding tools at a fundamental level, because that’s usually where the real leverage is.

Outside of ML, I spend time thinking about systems more broadly—economics, incentives, and how complex behavior emerges—and walking around the city to clear my head and reset my thinking.
